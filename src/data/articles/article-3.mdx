<Heading green='5 models' postGreen='That Likely Will Increase in Value' />

<Text black='1. Rolex Cosmograph Daytona 116500LN' />

<Text preBlack='The Rolex Cosmograph Daytona 116500LN stands out as an excellent choice for collectors seeking a modern Daytona with strong investment potential. Introduced in 2016, it marked the debut of the steel Daytona featuring a ceramic bezel. Its release generated significant anticipation, resulting in years-long waiting lists at authorized dealers that persist even today.' />

<Text preBlack='While various markets experienced declines, the Daytona market, although affected, did not plummet to the same extent as others. Notably, Daytonas are rarely found selling at their retail price due to persistent high demand, leading to considerable price hikes. As of January 2023, the retail price for this model is $14,800, but the average market values range from $27,900 to $33,100—an impressive appreciation from its retail value. The ref. 116500 reached its peak in 2022 at $48,500.' />

<SingleImage image='article3-10.png' />

<Text preBlack="Although experiencing a decline of over $2000 in the last 6 months and a more substantial decrease over the past year, the model is showing signs of a resurgence. This trend supports experts' observations that Daytonas and other top Rolex timepieces remain resilient assets in the market." />

<Text black='2. Rolex Submariner Steel ref 16610' />

<Text
  preBlack="The Rolex Submariner stands as an iconic and highly coveted timepiece globally, known for its timeless appeal. In continuous production since 1954, this classic watch remains a favorite among collectors, both seasoned and novice, showcasing enduring popularity. Worn by notable figures like Sean Connery and Michael Phelps, the Submariner maintains its status as a must-have in any watch collection.
The ref. 16610, produced from 1988 to 2010, represents a pivotal Submariner Date model, retaining the allure of the aluminum bezel insert and classic case proportions. Subsequent models adopted the robust Super Case, Cerachrom ceramic bezel, and solid bracelet links as modern upgrades. Despite these advancements, the ref. 16610 holds a special place in enthusiasts' hearts when envisioning the quintessential 'Submariner'"
/>

<SingleImage image='article3-11.png' />

<Text black='3. The Patek Philippe Nautilus 5711' />

<Text preBlack="The introduction of the Nautilus in 1976, a steel sports watch priced similarly to a gold Patek Philippe, was groundbreaking. Designed by Gerald Genta, it remains one of Patek Philippe's most popular watches over 40 years later. The 5711, launched in 2006, revamped the Nautilus line with a larger size and a new Caliber 324 SC. With its distinctive design, impeccable finishing, and now-discontinued status, the Nautilus 5711 is considered one of the most desirable watches on the market, making it a compelling investment choice in the realm of Patek Philippe timepieces." />

<SingleImage image='article3-12.png' />

<Text black='4. Audemars Piguet Royal Oak' />

<Text
  preBlack="When asked about Audemars Piguet's most renowned watch models, the Audemars Piguet Royal Oak inevitably comes to mind. Unveiled at Basel World in April 1972, this luxury steel watch with an integrated bracelet was a bold departure in design.
Crafted by the renowned Swiss watch designer Gérald Genta, the Royal Oak quickly achieved international acclaim and remains an iconic timepiece. The design, inspired by a traditional diver's helmet, boasts an octagonal-shaped bezel secured by visible screws. The water-resistant gasket and an exclusive blue tapisserie motif on the dial add to its distinctive features.
In its time, the Royal Oak's 39 mm diameter case was considered large, yet its slim 7 mm depth set it apart as one of the slimmest models available. The integrated stainless steel bracelet completed its iconic design. Despite its £2,700 price tag, higher than most gold watches then, owning a well-preserved Royal Oak from the 1970s today could see its value appreciate to around £105,000.
This substantial appreciation in value extends across Audemars Piguet's luxury watch range, solidifying its reputation as one of the premier investment brands in the market."
/>

<Text black='5. Rolex Pepsi GMT Master II' />

<Text preBlack="Upon its initial launch, the Pepsi GMT stood out as one of Rolex's highly sought-after models. Despite the passing of decades since the introduction of the original Rolex Pepsi watches, the collection remains in high demand and ranks among the most challenging Rolex timepieces to secure from authorized dealers. Pre-owned listings for the 126710BLRO Pepsi reference command prices as high as $22,000, a significant premium compared to its brand-new price of $10,750 for the GMT-Master II." />

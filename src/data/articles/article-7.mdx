Patek Philippe, a name synonymous with luxury and precision, has etched its mark in the annals of Swiss watchmaking for over 180 years. Remaining privately owned throughout its illustrious journey, the brand stands as a paragon of enduring craftsmanship and innovation.'

<SingleImage image='article7-2.png' />

<Heading preGreen='A Glimpse into' green='History' />

<Text preBlack='Founded in 1839 as Patek, Czapek & Cie, the brand commenced its journey crafting pocket watches for royalty. The pivotal meeting between Antoine Patek and Adrien Philippe in 1844 marked the inception of Patek Philippe. Officially named Patek, Philippe and Cie in 1851, the duo revolutionized watchmaking with the keyless winding and hand-setting system.' />

<MdxImages firstImage='article7-3.png' secondImage='article7-4.png' />

<Heading preGreen='International' green='Expansion' />

<Text preBlack="Venturing beyond Swiss borders in 1851, Patek Philippe showcased its pocket watches at the Great Exhibition in London. Notably, Queen Victoria's acquisition of a pendant watch marked the brand's foray into designing exquisite timepieces for women. A significant chapter unfolded in 1851 when Patek established ties with Charles Lewis Tiffany in New York, a partnership that endures, with Tiffany & Co. being the sole American retailer with its name on a Patek Philippe dial." />

<MdxImages firstImage='article7-5.png' secondImage='article7-6.png' />

<Heading preGreen='Technological' green='Marvels' />

<Text preBlack="Adrien Philippe's technical prowess shone in 1863 with the creation of the first pocket watch featuring a tourbillon, countering the effects of gravity on movement. The brand continued to innovate, filing numerous patents, including precision regulators, perpetual calendar mechanisms, and self-winding mechanisms." />

<MdxImages firstImage='article7-7.png' secondImage='article7-8.png' />

<Heading preGreen='Passing' green='the Torch' />

<Text preBlack="As the years unfolded, Patek Philippe seamlessly transitioned through generations. Emile Joseph, son of Adrien Philippe, played a crucial role in training under the family's legacy. The collaboration with Henry Graves, Jr. led to the creation of the iconic Henry Graves Supercomplication pocketwatch, a testament to the brand's commitment to excellence." />

<MdxImages firstImage='article7-9.png' secondImage='article7-10.png' />

<Heading preGreen='Stern Family' green='Era' />

<Text preBlack='In 1932, the Stern brothers, Jean and Charles Henri, acquired Patek Philippe, solidifying their bond with the brand. The Sterns introduced new collections, including Calatrava, Ellipse, Nautilus, Aquanaut, and Twenty~4, propelling Patek Philippe to new heights. Successively, Henri Stern, Philippe Stern, and Thierry Stern led the brand into the 21st century, maintaining its position at the pinnacle of watchmaking standards.' />

<MdxImages firstImage='article7-11.png' secondImage='article7-12.png' />

<Heading preGreen='Milestones in' green='History' />

<Text preBlack='Throughout its rich history, Patek Philippe achieved significant milestones, from the production of perpetual calendar watches in 1941 to the launch of the ultra-thin perpetual calendar reference in 1985. The brand consistently pushed boundaries, introducing the Sky Moon Tourbillon in 2001 and embracing silicon-based innovations.' />

<MdxImages firstImage='article7-13.png' secondImage='article7-14.png' />

<Heading preGreen='Unveiling' green='Little-Known Facts' />

<Text preBlack="Delving into the lesser-known facets of Patek Philippe reveals intriguing details, such as the brand's ventures into electronic movements before the quartz crisis and its commitment to producing all components in-house. The first Patek Philippe minute repeater wristwatch for a woman in 1916 and the establishment of the Advanced Research Department in 2002 underscore the brand's commitment to tradition and innovation." />

<Heading preGreen='A Lasting' green='Legacy' />

<Text preBlack='With fewer than one million watches produced since 1839, Patek Philippe stands as a pinnacle of exclusivity. Owning a Patek Philippe transcends time; it is a symbol of tradition, innovation, and an unwavering commitment to horological excellence.' />
